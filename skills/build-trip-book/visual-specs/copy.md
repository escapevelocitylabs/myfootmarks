### Neighbourhoods-at-a-glance
Six neighbourhood entries, each with:
- Name + character chars (e.g. `Moorish · Vertical · Fado`)
- Prose sentence
- `Walk this` anchor
- `Pairs with` anchor

**Alfama** — character: *Moorish · Vertical · Fado*
> The Moorish quarter the earthquake could not reach. Narrow, vertical, full of laundry lines and cats. You will get lost.
- Walk this: home base → Miradouro Sta Luzia → Castelo → Largo do Chafariz de Dentro (Fado Museum)
- Pairs with: a fado dinner, an hour at the Fado Museum first

**Baixa & Chiado** — *Grid · Rebuilt · Bohemian*
> The rebuilt downtown — grid of squares and pastry shops — and the bohemian shoulder above it where Pessoa wrote.
- Walk this: Praça da Figueira → Rua Augusta → Chiado → Carmo → Sta Justa down → Baixa
- Pairs with: Time Out Market lunch, bookshop-and-tile crawl

**Belém** — *River · Monuments · Pastries*
> A mile down the river, where the Age of Discoveries set sail. The monastery is a stone novel; the tower, a honeyed chess piece on the water.

**Bairro Alto & Príncipe Real** — *Quiet-by-day · Loud-by-night*
> Narrow-staircase bars, concept stores. By 2 AM a party spills out and is usually singing.

**Parque das Nações** — *Modern · Waterfront · Kid-heavy*
> The Expo '98 waterfront. Wide boulevards, cable cars, the Oceanário. Antidote if cobblestones start to win.

**Sintra (west)** — *Gardens · Palaces · Mist*
> 40-min train into the hills where royalty built strange things. Gardens designed for mist.

### Pullquote
> "You feel, in Lisbon, that you are always arriving somewhere — **even when you are only crossing a square.**"

### Food-scene note
> Bacalhau with a recipe for every day of the year. Pastéis de nata in every café. Spot prawns in season. Petiscos over vinho verde. Ginjinha in a small chocolate cup you can bite after.

### Thin-primer fallback (zero-research state)
> Lisbon — capital of Portugal, seven hills above the Tagus. Historic Alfama, Baixa, and Belém cluster near the water; Parque das Nações sits east, Sintra is a short train ride west.

### Weather strip (per-day cards)
- Thu · 23 — Partly sunny, breeze off the Tagus — 18°
- Fri · 24 — Sunny, no wind on the river — 21°
- Sat · 25 — Light rain likely — jackets for Sintra — 14°
- Sun · 26 — Sunny, long evening light — 20°
- Mon · 27 — Overcast, cool for flight day — 17°

### Weather lede
> Shoulder-season warmth with one damp day in the middle — lucky timing, since Sintra is best in mist. Evenings cool to 12 °C. Pack layers and a real rain jacket for the 25th.

### Event type labels
- `Dated · one-off`
- `Recurring · weekly`
- `Festival · multi-day`

### Dated event
**Dia da Liberdade — Freedom Day** (Sat 25, Holiday tag)
> Portugal's national holiday. Parade up Av. da Liberdade from ~16:00. Shops and museums run on holiday hours. The kids will remember the red carnations.

### Recurring event (weekly grid chips)
Tue: **Feira da Ladra flea market** · Campo de Santa Clara · 09:00–17:00
Sat: **Feira da Ladra (twice-weekly)** · Overlaps Freedom Day

### Recurring intro
> Recurring markets and open-air shows on the weekly schedule. Feira da Ladra runs Tue and Sat — during your trip that's Tue 23 (arrival day) and Sat 25.

### Festival
**IndieLisboa · Independent Film Festival**
Run: Thu 23 → Sun 04 May · overlaps your whole trip
> Portugal's main independent cinema festival, at Cinema São Jorge and venues across the city. English subtitles on most screenings. Family strand ("IndieJúnior") has afternoon slots.

Highlights:
- Main venue: Cinema São Jorge · Av. da Liberdade 175
- Tickets: Book same-day via indielisboa.com
- Family strand: IndieJúnior · weekend matinees
- Walk from home base: ~25 min, or tram

### Things to know about this week (6 entries)
- **In season → Jacarandas begin** — First violet flowers along the Avenida da Liberdade — it's early for full bloom but the first trees are turning. Worth walking the Avenida in late afternoon.
- **Reduced hours → Sat 25 holiday schedule** — Pena Palace and Jerónimos run reduced Freedom Day hours. Book the earliest Pena slot if you're in Sintra that day.
- **Free evening → MAAT free Sunday** — MAAT is free to enter every Sunday through the last hour. Your Sun 26 city day already includes a downtown walk — 30-min detour worth considering.
- **Book ahead → Ramiro Sunday fills fast** — Cervejaria Ramiro on Sun 26 at 19:00 is the busiest Sunday-night slot of the month. Book before arrival.
- **Weather-sensitive → Sintra Sat is the rain day** — Forecast is showing the 25th as the wet day. Sintra was literally designed for mist — the palaces and gardens look best in light rain. Plan it as-is; jackets ready.
- **New opening → Fado-na-Rua season** — Open-air fado performances begin in Alfama courtyards this week — low-key, unannounced, a reason to wander after 21:00.

## v2 — Three-doc split copy catalogs

Introduced by the three-file split (itinerary / checklists / keepsake) — see spec `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-24-three-file-trip-book-design.md`. All strings below are additive to the v1 catalogs above.

### Doc-rail pattern (shared by all three covers)

Every cover carries a `cover-rail` with `doc-mark` (doc type identifier) + `doc-desc` (one-line purpose). The rail is what identifies which of the three docs the reader is holding.

| Doc | `doc-mark` | `doc-desc` |
|---|---|---|
| Itinerary | `Itinerary` | `Bring this one with you · Day-by-day` |
| Checklists | `Checklists` | `Print this one · Cross off over time` |
| Keepsake | `Keepsake` | `Read this one ahead · Or leave it at the hotel` |

### Footer attribution (itinerary + keepsake)

Rendered at the end of `trip-itinerary.html` and `trip-book.html`:

```
<Doc mark> · <Destination> · <Dates> · This document is one of three.
```

Does NOT contain `<a href>` linking to the other docs — the footer is cross-file-link-free by design.
